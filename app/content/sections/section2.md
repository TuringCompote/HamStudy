# Operating and Procedures

Good operating is mostly convention: shared procedures that let strangers
exchange information cleanly under noise. Most of this section is vocabulary and
habit rather than theory.

**Identifying and calling.** Identify with your call sign at the start and end of
an exchange and periodically during it. On VHF/UHF you'll mostly work **simplex**
(direct) or through a **repeater** (listen on the output, transmit on the input —
the offset and tone are part of knowing a repeater). To join an ongoing
conversation, drop your call sign in a gap rather than saying "break" unless you
have priority traffic.

**The phonetic alphabet** (Alpha, Bravo, Charlie …) exists to make call signs and
spellings survive a weak, noisy path. Use it whenever accuracy matters.

**Signal reporting.** Voice uses the **RST** system — Readability (1–5),
Strength (1–9), and Tone (CW only). The S-meter reads strength, where roughly
**6 dB ≈ one S-unit**. **Q-codes** (QTH = location, QSY = change frequency, QRM =
interference, QSB = fading, etc.) compress common phrases into three letters and
came from CW practice.

**Courtesy and care.** Listen before transmitting, keep a dummy load handy for
tune-ups so you don't radiate while adjusting, leave gaps for others, and keep a
station log of your contacts. In nets and emergencies, follow net-control
direction and keep transmissions short.
