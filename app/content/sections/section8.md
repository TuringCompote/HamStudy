# Interference and Suppression

This section is about unwanted energy — getting into equipment that shouldn't
receive it, or leaving your transmitter when it shouldn't. Know the mechanism,
its symptom, and the fix.

**Interference to other equipment.** **Front-end overload** happens when your
strong nearby signal swamps a receiver's input stage — even gear that isn't tuned
to you; the fix is at the *victim* (filtering, better selectivity). **Audio
rectification** is when RF is detected by audio equipment (stereos, phones) and
comes out as sound; cure it with bypass capacitors and ferrite chokes on the
affected leads.

**Unwanted emissions from your transmitter.** **Harmonics** are multiples of your
operating frequency; **spurious emissions** are any output outside the intended
channel; **splatter** is excess bandwidth from overmodulation or overdriving;
**key-clicks** come from switching a CW signal too abruptly. The cures are at the
*source*: don't overdrive, shape keying, and add **low-pass / band-pass filters**
so only the wanted signal reaches the antenna.

**Intermodulation** occurs when two signals mix in a non-linear stage and produce
new frequencies — addressed by improving linearity and filtering.

**Resolving complaints.** Canada uses **EMCAB-2** field-strength criteria as the
yardstick for immunity. Approach interference complaints methodically and
courteously: confirm the symptom, identify whether the problem is at the source or
the victim, apply the matching filter or bypass, and document what you did.
